System Architecture

The REUSALIA platform — an end-to-end pipeline that moves data from public marketplaces, through processing and a custom ERP core, out to live storefronts and physical hardware on the warehouse floor.

Everything routes through a single source of truth. Data sources feed a processing layer, which writes to the ERP core, which in turn drives outputs — from marketplace listings to label printers running on local hardware.

About & Resilience

In October 2024 a catastrophic flood hit. We rebuilt the company — and the systems that run it — from the ground up.

I founded REUSALIA at 25. Three years on it's a profitable, growing operation with a 5-person team, running entirely on infrastructure I designed and built alone — self-taught, iterating in production. I lead the team, teach each person their role, then delegate, review, correct, and iterate again.

At REUSALIA I architected and built the entire platform: a custom ERP backed by PostgreSQL, marketplace extraction engines that process roughly 100 orders a day, an AI enrichment pipeline that saves around 40 hours every week, and a cloud-to-local bridge that drives physical label printers on the warehouse floor.

Then, in October 2024, a catastrophic flood wiped out operations. Rather than walk away, we rebuilt — the warehouse, the team, and the software stack. That recovery is the part of my story I'm proudest of: it proved the systems were sound and the team was resilient. I don't just design architecture diagrams; I'm the founder who ships the code that brings them back online.
